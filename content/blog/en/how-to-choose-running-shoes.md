# How to Choose Running Shoes: A Practical Guide for Beginners

The right running shoes help you stay consistent, comfortable, and injury-free.

From our experience coaching hundreds of beginner runners in Mexico City parks, the biggest comfort upgrade was not the plan itself, it was getting the right pair of shoes.

## What matters most in running shoes

The best running shoes balance fit, cushioning, and stability for your body and your routes.

You do not need the most expensive model, you need the pair that keeps you running without pain week after week.

## How to spot your gait without a lab

Your gait is the way your foot lands and pushes off the ground.

You can estimate it by checking the wear pattern of your current shoes and how your ankles and knees feel after easy runs.

- **Neutral:** even wear, no inward collapse.
- **Overpronation:** inner wear, occasional inner-knee or ankle discomfort.
- **Supination:** outer wear, tighter ankles and calves.

If you are unsure, send a photo of the sole and a short description of your discomfort.

## Shoe types and when to use them

Running shoes are built for different jobs: daily training, speed work, and racing.

Choosing the right type prevents you from training in a shoe that is too aggressive or too soft for your volume.

- **Daily trainers:** stable, cushioned, ideal for most beginner runs.
- **Tempo shoes:** lighter and more responsive for intervals or fartlek.
- **Race shoes:** very light, not ideal as your only pair when starting.

If you are new, prioritize comfort and stability over speed.

## Fit, lacing, and toe room

Proper fit prevents hot spots, black toenails, and unnecessary pain.

It also helps you stay consistent during your first 8 to 12 weeks of training.

- Leave a thumb-width of space in the toe box.
- Lock the heel with the top eyelets.
- Try shoes later in the day when feet are slightly swollen.

Bring your usual socks and any insoles when you try new shoes.

## When to replace your shoes

Worn-out cushioning increases impact and can trigger new aches.

Replacing shoes on time is cheaper than recovering from a preventable injury.
