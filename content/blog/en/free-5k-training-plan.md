## Why pick the 5K distance?

The 5K is the safest and most motivating distance for beginners.

It builds fitness fast without overwhelming your joints or schedule.

- Manageable duration: 25-35 minutes for most beginners.
- Lower injury risk thanks to progressive mileage.
- Confidence builder for longer races.
- Endless racing calendar across Mexico and Latin America.

## Proven eight-week benefits

An eight-week structure gives your body enough time to adapt without overload.

The goal is to run consistently, not to chase pace too early.

- Up to 25% cardiovascular improvement.
- 40% reduction in perceived stress and anxiety.
- Better sleep quality and higher self-esteem.
- Average weight loss between 3 and 5 kilograms.

## Complete training calendar

This calendar alternates jog and walk blocks to build endurance safely.

If you feel overly tired, reduce volume and keep the habit intact.

### Weeks 1-2: Adaptation

- Monday: 30 minutes with 1 min jog / 2 min walk repeats.
- Wednesday: 25-30 minutes varying easy efforts.
- Friday: 30-35 minutes with 1.5-2 min jog / 2 min walk intervals.
- Saturday: 35-40 minute brisk walk.
- Remaining days: rest, stretching, or mobility.

### Weeks 3-4: Aerobic build

- Monday: 8-12 minute jog blocks with short breaks.
- Wednesday: strength for runners + 15 minute walk.
- Friday: 40-45 minute long easy run.
- Saturday: relaxed jog based on how you feel.

### Weeks 5-6: Specific endurance

- Monday: 35 minutes with 15-20 minute continuous sections.
- Tuesday: gentle intervals (2 min strong / 2 min easy).
- Wednesday: continuous 30 minute jog.
- Saturday: 40-45 minute easy long run.

### Weeks 7-8: Race prep

- Week 7: easy 5K test plus recovery-focused runs.
- Week 8: volume taper, three very easy jogs, race on Saturday.

## Quick prevention checklist

Short warm-ups and cool-downs reduce common beginner injuries.

- Dynamic warm-up (5 minutes): progressive walk, hip circles, leg swings, light skips.
- Cool-down (8 minutes): walk, calf stretch, quad stretch, hamstring stretch.

## Smart fueling for your 5K

Simple nutrition habits are enough to feel strong during a 5K plan.

- Pre-run: light carbs 2-3 hours before and solid hydration.
- During: water only for workouts under one hour.
- Post-run: rehydrate and mix carbs with protein within 30 minutes.
